Amphibians

All amphibians are vertebrates, and they need moist environments or water to survive.

They are cold-blooded. They absorb water and breathe through their thin skin. They have at least one special skin gland used for defense.

Birds

Birds are a type of warm-blooded vertebrate that are adapted to fly.

Not all birds can fly, but they do all have wings. Birds have beaks that help them catch and swallow food. The digestive system of a bird allows it to eat whenever it can and digest the food later.
